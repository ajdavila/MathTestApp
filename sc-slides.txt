Slide 2 — Why Now?
Expanding data footprint: model features, intelligence sources, PII/PCI adjacency

Faster delivery cycles: analytics sprints, rapid model deployments

Regulatory and stakeholder expectations: auditability, explainability, access governance

Threat landscape: data exfiltration, supply-chain, prompt/data poisoning, shadow workflows

Speaker Notes: Our risk increases with data volume, velocity, and dependency on third-party tooling. A champion model embeds security decisions where work happens—sprints, code reviews, and data operations.

Slide 3 — What Is a Security Champion?
A trusted member of the team with interest and insight in security

Amplifies secure practices in the squad

Supports security across the lifecycle: requirements → build → test → release

Champion is a force multiplier inside the squad, not the security team.

Liaison between the team and central security/risk/governance and CISo/BISO

Enabler of secure design, not a blocker or auditor

Time-bound role (10/20% capacity), with defined backlog items and KPIs

Speaker Notes: A Champion is a multiplier: they surface risks early, coach peers, and keep us aligned with policy while preserving speed.

Slide 4 — What a Champion Is Not
Not the only person responsible for security (everyone owns it)
Not the sole owner of security or an approvals gate
Not a replacement for central security, legal, privacy, or audit
Not a full-time role or a security “janitor” for the team

Speaker Notes: Everyone still owns security. The Champion coordinates—and escalates thoughtfully when needed.

Slide 5 — What Does a Security Champion Do?
Education and sharing: curate guidance and bring it to standups or show-and-tells
Sprint hygiene: ensure security acceptance criteria are added where relevant, ensure scans are reviewed and fixes are understood
Escalation point: route questions and incidents early to CISO/BISO
Keep inform the team with recent vulnerabilities or security actions.

FAQ

How much time does a Champion need?
Plan 10–20% of capacity. Concentrate on embedding controls into existing workflows to minimize overhead.


What if scans block delivery?
Use risk-based waivers with time-bound exceptions and remediation owners. Champions coordinate with security to tune false positives.


Does every squad need a Champion?
Yes—coverage should follow delivery lines. Smaller squads can share one, but ensure clear ownership and backups.
